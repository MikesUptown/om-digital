01. designing nyc public health intervention

Working with a division of the NYC Department of Health our team was asked to create overall branding for a public health intervention targeting young women in targeted NYC neighborhoods. The intervention included an online feedback tool that participants would be asked to log into and asnwer questions.

We conducted several one-on-one interviews with individuals from the research team and the target communities. We created multiple versions of the logo and branding, using custom fonts, different color palettes and layout styles. One deliverable we handed off were comprehensive branding guidelines and messaging for the project including look-and-feel for all front-end elements of the online intervention tool and printed materials.

02. mapping community resources

We collaborated with a research group at Mailman School of Public Health at Columbia U. to geocode and design a map of community resources in upper Manhattan and parts of the South Bronx. The map was part of a NIH-financed study targeting lower-income communities who may not have access to traditional healthcare services to determine if they would utilize the services.

This project turned out to be a great success. To make it work we used the Mapbox API which is powered by open data from OpenStreetMap. We customized all features of the map whichincluded resources like food pantries, drug treatment centers and housing resources. Categories were grouped by color coded markers with custom map symbols. We managed the data from start to finish and geocoded over 100 available resources. During interviews users noted its ease-of-use, navigation elements and design aesthetics.

03. process for user interface

For a text-message based intervention our research team was asked to outline a process by which users would sign-up for a four week informational campaign to study the impact of text messages on patient adherence. Our task was to come up with a secure process that protected patient information while encouraging potential users to not bail during the sign-up process.

We worked through multiple iterations of the sign-up process, from initial recruitment through completion of the text campaign, including follow-up interviews. We tested multiple scenarios along the way to improve the process. The result... we delivered a detailed user interface process that included a simple, secure sign-up process that protected patient data while reducing overall study attrition.

04. managing complex datasets

For an ad agency collaborator we were asked to program an online assessment tool in the form of a questionnaire. The questionnaire had a detailed skip pattern and the end result for respondents would be a customized recommendation based on responses.

With complex data projects like this we have found that it's important to set up a clear process to manage updates to the overall module, using version control systems like Git ∧ Github. In this way we are able to reduce the time it takes to get a working demo; and then push regular updates as the overall module is improved.
