our work: samples

We have worked on some really interesting projects. These include design and development projects; we also have done a few mapping projects; prototyping. Also the lost art of content and writing is a strength of ours.

01. branding & design of nyc public health intervention

Working with a division of the NYC Department of Health our team was chosen to create overall branding for a public health intervention targeting young women in key NYC neighborhoods. The unique project is for an intervention using an online feedback tool.

To complete the branding our design team conducted several one-on-one interviews with individuals from the research team. We created different versions of the logo and branding, using multiple fonts, colors and layout styles which were then tested with target audiences. The result of this work were branding guidelines for the project that included guidance for all front-end elements of the online intervention tool.

02. mapping community resources

We recently collaborated with researchers at the Mailman School of Public Health at CUMC to design and geocode a map of community resources in upper Manhattan and parts of the Bronx. The map was part of a NIH-financed study targeting lower-income communities who may not have access to traditional healthcare services.

For this project we used the Mapbox API which is powered by open data from OpenStreetMap. Map categories included resources like food pantries, drug treatment centers and housing resources which we grouped by color coded markers and custom map symbols. We managed map data from start to finish and geocoded over 100 available resources. Feedback from users noted its ease-of-use, design aesthetics and map navigation elements.

03. designing process for user interface research

For a text-message based intervention our research team was asked to outline a process by which users would sign-up for a four week informational campaign to study the impact of text messages for patient adherence. Our key challenge was to design a secure process that protected patient information.

Our team worked through multiple iterations of the sign-up process, from initial recruitment through completion of the text campaign, including follow-up interviews; we tested multiple scenarios along the way to improve the process. The result, we delivered a detailed user interface process that included a simple, secure sign-up process that protected patient data while reducing overall study attrition.
